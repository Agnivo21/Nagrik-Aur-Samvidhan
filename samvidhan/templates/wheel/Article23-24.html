Fundamental Rights: Articles 23-24

Article 23: Protection Against Exploitation

Article 23 of the Indian Constitution, part of the Fundamental Rights, addresses the prohibition of exploitation. It specifically outlaws the trafficking of human beings, which includes practices like forced labor and slavery. Additionally, it prohibits "begar," a term referring to forced or unpaid labor. This article is designed to protect individuals from exploitation and ensure that labor practices are fair and just, safeguarding human dignity and freedom.

Article 24: Prohibition of Child Labor

Article 24 of the Indian Constitution addresses the prohibition of child labor. It specifically bans the employment of children under the age of 14 in factories, mines, and other hazardous occupations. This provision aims to protect children from exploitation and ensure their right to education and a healthy, safe environment. The goal is to prevent the abuse of child labor and to safeguard children's physical and mental development, promoting their well-being and future opportunities.

Case Example : Bandhua Mukti Morcha vs. Union of India (1984).

In 1984, the Supreme Court of India, led by Justice P.N. Bhagwati, delivered a landmark judgment. The court recognized the widespread existence of bonded labor and ordered the immediate release and rehabilitation of the laborers and took rehabilitative measures.
