Fundamental Rights: Articles 32-35

Article 14: Right to Equality

Everyone is equal in the eyes of the law. The government cannot discriminate against anyone based on religion, race, caste, gender, or place of birth.

Article 15: Prohibition of Discrimination

No one can be discriminated against on grounds of religion, race, caste, gender, or place of birth. However, the state can make special provisions for women, children, and backward classes.

Article 16: Equality of Opportunity in Public Employment

All citizens have an equal opportunity to work in government jobs. There can be no discrimination based on religion, race, caste, gender, or place of birth. However, the government can reserve some jobs for certain backward classes to promote equality.

Article 17: Abolition of Untouchability

Untouchability is abolished, and it is forbidden to practice it in any form. Anyone who practices untouchability can be punished by law.

Article 18: Abolition of Titles

The state cannot give any titles of nobility to citizens. Titles like 'Sir' or 'Raja' are not allowed. However, military and academic honors can still be given.
