Introduction to the Preamble

The Preamble outlines India’s core principles: sovereignty, socialism, secularism, democracy, and republicanism. It emphasizes justice, liberty, equality, and fraternity.

Directive Principles of State Policy

The Directive Principles of State Policy guide the government in creating policies for social, economic, and political justice. They aim to promote the welfare of the people but are not legally enforceable.

Fundamental
Duties

The Fundamental Duties, outline citizens’ Fundamental Duties which include respecting the flag, promoting harmony, protecting public property, striving for excellence, and fostering civic consciousness and societal welfare.

Fundamental
Rights

Fundamental Rights, safeguard individual liberties and freedoms. They protect against state actions and include equality, free speech, the right to life, and protection from discrimination.

Discover the journey of India’s Constitution:
Constituent Assembly: Formed in 1946 with 389 members, later 299 after partition.
Drafting Committee: Led by Dr. B.R. Ambedkar, included top leaders and experts.
Debates: 11 sessions over 165 days to shape the final document.
Adoption: Adopted on Nov 26, 1949, effective from Jan 26, 1950 (Republic Day).
Influences:Inspired by British, American, Irish, and French constitutions.
